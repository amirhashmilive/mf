Bolti Nadi: Speaking for Waters

A flagship river rejuvenation and clean-water campaign impacting over 20 million lives through community-led ecological restoration.

SDG 6: Clean Water & Sanitation Est. 2016

Sakri River Walk: A Landmark Achievement

In 2019, the Bolti Nadi initiative successfully executed a historic 90-kilometer on-foot expedition along the Sakri River. Over four continuous days, our team traversed the landscape from the river's origin in Kariyama Village (Kawardha) to its confluence with the Half River in Darhi Village (Bemetara).

Key Metrics & Impact

  • 90km Foot Expedition across 2 districts
  • Water samples collected and analyzed from every bankside village
  • 59 villages provided with improved water flow through desilting
  • 2 Crore INR Sewage Treatment Plant established at Kawardha

Methodology: Science meets Community

The mission was designed as a multi-dimensional research project. We didn't just walk; we documented water quality parameters for drinking and agricultural use, assessed the river’s influence on local livestock, and mapped bank erosion at critical junctions like Rajanawagaon and Kodar.

Our team also recognized rivers as repositories of civilization, documenting heritage sites like the 14th-century Sherki Mahal and the 11th-century Bhoramdev Temple. This holistic approach ensured that our rejuvenation recommendations respected both ecology and culture.

From Research to Policy Action

The research report submitted to district administrations led to the Mukhyamantri Vriksh Sampada Yojana planting commercial and medicinal trees along riverbanks. MGNREGA labor teams were mobilized for silt removal, and check dams were constructed at erosion-prone sites to prevent further land loss.

Today, the Bolti Nadi documentary serves as an educational tool, screened in over 50 countries and at institutions across Chhattisgarh, demonstrating the power of grassroots research in catalyzing government collaboration.

Core Objectives

Hydrological Assessment

Continuous monitoring of PH levels and quality indices.

Community Engagement

Direct consultations with riparian dwellers on water challenges.

Ecosystem Restoration

Integrated rejuvenation through desilting and plantation.
