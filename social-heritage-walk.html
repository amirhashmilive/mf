Social Heritage Walk

Connecting the present with the glorious past of Chhattisgarh to preserve our unique archaeological and living culture.

SDG 11: Sustainable Cities & Communities April 18th Events

Preserving Our Living History

The Social Heritage Walk is an initiative by Meer Foundation designed to foster a deeper connection between citizens and the rich cultural landscape of Chhattisgarh. We believe that heritage is not just in museumsâ€”it is in the stones of our temples, the stories of our elders, and the rhythm of our rivers.

Every year on **April 18th (World Heritage Day)**, we organize mass-participation walks that bring together students, historians, and local residents. Under the hashtag **#LocalHeritage**, we document forgotten stories and advocate for the preservation of overlooked archaeological sites.

Digital Documentation

Using GIS and high-res photography to create a digital map of Chhattisgarh's heritage.

Institutional Support

Collaborating with the Department of Archaeology to protect endangered sites.

Beyond the Walk

Our walks focus on more than just viewingâ€”they are teaching moments. We cover everything from the 11th-century Bhoramdev Temple complex to the unique iron-smelting traditions of the Agaria community. By involving the youth, we ensure that the next generation values and protects the cultural wealth of their homeland.
